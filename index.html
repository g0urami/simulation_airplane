Aviation Physics Theory

Bernoulli's Principle

When the velocity of a fluid increases, its pressure decreases. This principle is crucial for generating lift on aircraft wings.

Newton's Laws of Motion

Aircraft movement is explained by Newton's three laws of motion. The action-reaction principle is particularly important for thrust generation.

Four Forces of Flight

Learn about the four main forces acting on an aircraft: lift, drag, thrust, and gravity, and how their balance affects flight.

Simulation Controls Guide

Basic Controls

  • Speed Control: Use the slider to adjust aircraft speed.
  • Altitude Control: Use the slider to adjust aircraft altitude.
  • Angle of Attack: Use the slider to adjust the wing's angle of attack.

Environment Settings

  • Wind Speed: Use the slider to adjust wind intensity.
  • Aircraft Model: Select different aircraft models from the dropdown menu.

Camera Controls

  • Free Camera: Click and drag to rotate, scroll to zoom, right-click to pan.
  • Cockpit View: Experience the simulation from the pilot's perspective.
  • Follow Aircraft: Camera automatically follows the aircraft movement.

About This Simulation

This aircraft physics simulation is designed as an educational tool for students learning about aerodynamics and physics principles. It provides a visual and interactive way to understand how aircraft fly and the physical forces involved.

The simulation uses Three.js for 3D rendering and implements realistic physics calculations to demonstrate aerodynamic principles in action.

Educational Value

Designed specifically for physics education, with accurate representations of aerodynamic principles.

Interactive Learning

Students can adjust parameters in real-time and observe the effects on flight dynamics.

Visual Representation

3D visualization helps students understand abstract physics concepts in a tangible way.
